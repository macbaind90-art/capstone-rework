My Transition into Tech

Why I’m moving from security operations into the technology world.

I’ve always been interested in technology, but only recently had the opportunity to fully pursue it. After years of building and leading training programs, managing complex operations, and solving real-world problems, I see tech as the next evolution of that same mission—using tools, data, and code to make systems smarter and people safer.

My goal is to take the mindset I developed in the Army and in security operations—structured thinking, tactical awareness, and calm under pressure—and apply it to software, cybersecurity, and modern IT environments.

Transitioning out of the military and deep into security work taught me how to operate when the stakes are high and the path isn’t always clear. The hardest part of shifting into tech has been balancing responsibility to my family with the time and focus needed to learn new skills. It’s a real challenge—but it’s also exactly why I’m committed to this path.

I’m approaching tech the same way I approached training and operations: with a plan, measurable goals, and a commitment to continuous improvement.
